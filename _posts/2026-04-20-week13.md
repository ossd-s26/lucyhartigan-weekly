## Open Everything!

This week in class we discussed how Open Source Software Development principles can be applied into other places. While some examples still seemed to be very software heavy, there were lots of examples that demonstrated desires to improve accesibility to education, medical information, or environmental information.

My example was for a platform that allows bird watchers to log their data. The *open* part of this, is that this data is openly accessible and often used by researchers to identify migration patterns or work on conservation efforts. Some other interesting examples were OpenLung, which improves accessibility in medicine, and Open Educational Resources which is improving education accessibility. I don't think I found any projects I would use (other than the open textbook resource, I wish I had that three years ago! ), but it was still interesting to see how varied these Open projects could be.
